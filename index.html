Tribute Page - VIRENDER SEHWAG

About Virender Sehwag

Virender Sehwag is a renowned former Indian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the most destructive opening batsmen in the history of the sport. Born on October 20, 1978, in Delhi, India, Sehwag made his international debut in 1999 and went on to have a remarkable career that spanned over a decade. Known for his aggressive batting style and fearless approach, Sehwag became a fan favorite and left an indelible mark on the game.

Throughout his career, Sehwag achieved numerous records and accolades. He became the first Indian cricketer to score a triple century in Test cricket, accomplishing this feat twice in his career. Notably, he holds the record for the fastest triple century, reaching the milestone off just 278 balls. Sehwag also holds the record for the highest individual score by an Indian in One Day Internationals (ODIs), scoring a magnificent 219 runs against West Indies. His ability to dominate the opposition with his aggressive strokeplay and fearless mindset made him a force to be reckoned with on the cricket field.

Sehwag's impact on the game extended beyond his individual records. He played a crucial role in India's victory in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20 tournament and the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup. With his explosive batting at the top of the order, he provided blistering starts to the Indian team and set the tone for successful campaigns. His unique approach and ability to attack from the outset revolutionized the role of an opening batsman, inspiring a new generation of cricketers.

Career Highlights

  • First Indian to score a triple century in Test cricket
  • Fastest triple century in Test cricket history
  • One of four batsmen to have scored two triple centuries in Tests
  • Record for the highest individual score in One Day Internationals by an Indian
  • Scored a century in a single session of a Test match twice
